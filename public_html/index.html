Minimum Enclosing Sphere Problem

The Minimum Enclosing Sphere problem describes trying to find a new point that minimizes the maximum distance to every other point. In real life, this can be compared to a corporation trying to place its new headquarters such that it is as close as possible to all of its branch locations. The centerpoint plus the distance to the furthest point(s) creates a circle in 2 dimensions or a sphere in greater than two dimensions. Another way to think of this problem is that the circle created is as small as possible while still encompassing every point in the set.
